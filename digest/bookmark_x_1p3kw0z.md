## Summary

Sergey Levine suggests that training Vision-Language Models (VLAs) with diverse multimodal prompts—such as specific actions like grasping, moving, and placing objects—enables better control and success through detailed, step-by-step guidance. Powerful VLMs can then effectively steer these models toward achieving complex tasks by interpreting and executing such precise instructions.

**Category:** Model training and reinforcement learning

---

## Original Content

If we train VLAs to respond to diverse multimodal prompts, then we can steer them better: [grasp the carrot]/[move to x,y,z]/[put the carrot on the plate]. With many levels of detail, powerful VLMs can step in and steer the model to success much more often! More below 👇 pic.twitter.com/FJfIPx0xTc &mdash; Sergey Levine (@svlevine) February 16, 2026
