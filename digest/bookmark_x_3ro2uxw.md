## Summary

A new paper proposes training AI agents to reflexively flag their own misbehavior, akin to an involuntary muscle twitch, suggesting this self-monitoring could be more reliable than traditional alignment methods or blackbox oversight. The approach aims to enhance accountability without relying on complex training or external supervision.
