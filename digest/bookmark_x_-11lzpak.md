## Summary

A new Stanford+Caltech paper introduces a 2-axis taxonomy to address the gap between high benchmark scores and real-world reasoning flaws in LLMs, categorizing reasoning into embodied (physical interaction) and non-embodied (abstract) tasks. The framework highlights how non-embodied reasoning may mask deeper weaknesses in practical problem-solving.
