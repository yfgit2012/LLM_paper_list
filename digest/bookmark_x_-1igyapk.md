## Summary

A new study from Google and the University of Wisconsin reveals that large language models (LLMs) can reconstruct meaningful outputs from nonsensical words by leveraging grammar and contextual patterns, highlighting their ability to infer intent despite spelling errors. This finding underscores the significance of context in language processing and may influence how users approach prompt crafting.
