## Summary

Experiential Reinforcement Learning (ERL) enables LLMs to learn from experience by having agents attempt tasks, receive sparse feedback, reflect self-critically, and retry, with improvements distilled back into the base policy to solidify corrections without external intervention. This loop of trial, reflection, and policy refinement enhances learning efficiency.
