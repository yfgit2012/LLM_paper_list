## Summary
- **Objective**: To investigate teacher-student training frameworks (SOAR) with a focus on reward dynamics, diversity preservation, and the effectiveness of multi-turn sampling strategies in generating questions/answers. The study aims to compare intrinsic vs. external reward mechanisms (Intrinsic-T vs. Grounded-T) for maintaining diversity during training.  
- **Methodologies**: The paper employs SOAR, a teacher-student training framework, with reward dynamics modeled through cyclical search-exploitation phases. Diversity is measured using cosine distances between embeddings (e.g., EmbeddingGemma300M). Multi-turn sampling is compared against single-turn sampling, and training curves, hyperparameter ablation (e.g., `n ∈ {32, 64}`), and `Pass@k` metrics are analyzed across datasets.  
- **Results**:  
  - **Grounded-T** (external rewards) preserves diversity throughout training, while **Intrinsic-T** (self-reinforced rewards) collapses into a narrower conceptual space.  
  - Reward dynamics exhibit oscillations (search phase) and reward increases (exploitation phase), with student promotions triggered by thresholds (e.g., `τ = 0.01`).  
  - Multi-turn sampling underperforms compared to single-turn sampling in generating questions/answers.  
  - Training curves show fluctuations, and `Pass@k` results vary with sampling strategies and dataset sizes.  
- **Key Contributions**:  
  - Introduces SOAR as a framework for teacher-student training with explicit reward dynamics.  
  - Demonstrates that external rewards (Grounded-T) outperform self-reinforced rewards (Intrinsic-T) in preserving diversity, aligning with reinforcement learning principles.  
  - Highlights limitations of multi-turn sampling and provides empirical insights into diversity metrics and training behavior.  
- **Conclusions**: Grounded-T models maintain diversity and avoid conceptual collapse by leveraging external rewards, whereas intrinsic reward mechanisms lead to diversity loss. Multi-turn sampling is less effective than single-turn strategies. These findings advance understanding of reward design in teacher-student frameworks and reinforcement learning exploration.

## 摘要
- **目标**：研究师生训练框架（SOAR），重点探讨奖励动态、多样性保持以及多轮采样策略在生成问答对中的有效性。研究旨在比较内在奖励机制与外在奖励机制（Intrinsic-T vs. Grounded-T）在训练过程中保持多样性的作用。  
- **方法**：论文采用SOAR师生训练框架，通过循环的搜索-利用阶段建模奖励动态。使用嵌入向量之间的余弦距离（如EmbeddingGemma300M）衡量多样性。比较多轮采样与单轮采样，分析训练曲线、超参数消融实验（如`n ∈ {32, 64}`）及`Pass@k`指标在不同数据集上的表现。  
- **结果**：  
  - **Grounded-T**（外在奖励）在训练过程中保持多样性，而**Intrinsic-T**（自我强化奖励）则坍缩至更狭窄的概念空间。  
  - 奖励动态表现出搜索阶段的震荡和利用阶段的奖励提升，学生升级由阈值触发（如`τ = 0.01`）。  
  - 多轮采样在生成问答对时表现劣于单轮采样。  
  - 训练曲线存在波动，`Pass@k`结果随采样策略和数据集规模变化而变化。  
- **关键贡献**：  
  - 引入SOAR作为具有明确奖励动态的师生训练框架。  
  - 证明外在奖励（Grounded-T）在保持多样性方面优于自我强化奖励（Intrinsic-T），符合强化学习原理。  
  - 指出多轮采样的局限性，并提供关于多样性指标和训练行为的实证见解。  
- **结论**：Grounded-T模型通过利用外在奖励保持多样性，避免概念坍缩，而内在奖励机制导致多样性损失。多轮采样效果弱于单轮策略。这些发现推动了师生框架中奖励设计及强化学习探索的理解。
