### **1. Teacher Training Dynamics (SOAR with HARP)**
- **Cyclical Search-Exploitation Pattern**:  
  - The teacher follows a cycle of **search** (exploring diverse strategies) and **exploitation** (refining effective strategies).  
  - **Reward oscillations** occur during search phases, followed by a steady rise in rewards as the teacher exploits learned strategies.  
  - **Student promotions** (updating the student baseline) trigger reward drops, as older curricula become less relevant. The teacher then adapts to the improved baseline, leading to recovery in rewards.  

- **Promotion Mechanism**:  
  - Promotions are triggered when the **3-step moving average of teacher rewards** exceeds a threshold (τ = 0.01).  
  - This ensures the teacher adapts to the student’s progress, balancing exploration and exploitation.

---

### **2. Intrinsic-T vs. Grounded-T: Key Differences**
#### **Intrinsic-T (Intrinsic Rewards)**  
- **Reward Dynamics**:  
  - Smooth upward trend in rewards during training.  
  - **Loss of Diversity**: As rewards increase, the diversity of teacher completions decreases (measured by cosine distance of embeddings).  
  - **Convergence**: Teachers collapse into a narrow conceptual space, reducing the variety of generated questions/answers.  

- **Implications**:  
  - Over-reliance on intrinsic rewards may lead to **overfitting** or **conceptual narrowing**, limiting adaptability to diverse student needs.  

#### **Grounded-T (Grounded Rewards)**  
- **Reward Dynamics**:  
  - Maintains **stability** and **diversity** throughout training.  
  - Preserves the original model’s diversity, ensuring a broad range of teaching strategies.  

- **Implications**:  
  - Grounded rewards support **long-term adaptability** and **robustness**, aligning with findings in Table 1 (similar diversity to Base-T).  

---

### **3. Sampling Strategies: Single-Turn vs. Multi-Turn**
- **Single-Turn Sampling**:  
  - Generates questions and answers in a single step, with filtering to ensure format compliance.  
  - **Better Performance**: Achieves higher **Pass@k** scores (Table 8) compared to multi-turn methods.  

- **Multi-Turn Sampling**:  
  - Separates question generation and answer generation into distinct stages.  
  - **Underperformance**: Results in lower Pass@k scores, likely due to **inconsistent formatting** or **noisy outputs** without filtering.  

- **Conclusion**:  
  - Single-turn sampling is more **efficient and reliable** for maintaining quality, while multi-turn methods may require additional safeguards to avoid degradation in performance.

---

### **4. Practical Implications**
- **Grounded-T** is preferable for maintaining **diverse teaching strategies** and **long-term adaptability**, as it avoids convergence to a narrow conceptual space.  
- **Intrinsic-T** may be suitable for short-term tasks but risks **reduced diversity** and **overfitting** over time.  
- **Single-turn sampling** is recommended for consistency and quality, while multi-turn approaches require careful design to mitigate performance gaps.

### **1. 教师训练动态（SOAR with HARP）**
- **循环的探索-利用模式**：  
  - 教师遵循**探索**（探索多样化策略）和**利用**（优化有效策略）的周期。  
  - **奖励波动**出现在探索阶段，随后在教师利用所学策略时奖励趋于稳定上升。  
  - **学生升级**（更新学生基线）会引发奖励下降，因为旧课程变得不相关。教师随后适应改进后的基线，奖励随之恢复。  

- **升级机制**：  
  - 当**教师奖励的3步移动平均值**超过阈值（τ = 0.01）时触发升级。  
  - 这确保教师适应学生进步，平衡探索与利用。  

---

### **2. Intrinsic-T 与 Grounded-T 的关键差异**
#### **Intrinsic-T（内在奖励）**  
- **奖励动态**：  
  - 训练期间奖励呈现平滑上升趋势。  
  - **多样性丧失**：随着奖励增加，教师生成的完成内容多样性下降（通过嵌入向量的余弦距离衡量）。  
  - **收敛**：教师逐渐坍缩到狭窄的概念空间，减少生成问题/答案的多样性。  

- **影响**：  
  - 过度依赖内在奖励可能导致**过拟合**或**概念狭窄**，限制对多样化学生需求的适应能力。  

#### **Grounded-T（基于现实的奖励）**  
- **奖励动态**：  
  - 训练期间保持**稳定性**和**多样性**。  
  - 保留原始模型的多样性，确保广泛的教学策略。  

- **影响**：  
  - 基于现实的奖励支持**长期适应性**和**鲁棒性**，与表1中的发现一致（与Base-T相似的多样性）。  

---

### **3. 采样策略：单轮 vs 多轮**
- **单轮采样**：  
  - 单步生成问题和答案，并通过过滤确保格式合规。  
  - **表现更好**：相比多轮方法，实现更高的**Pass@k**分数（表8）。  

- **多轮采样**：  
  - 将问题生成和答案生成分为独立阶段。  
  - **表现不佳**：导致较低的Pass@k分数，可能由于**格式不一致**或**未过滤输出的噪声**。  

- **结论**：  
  - 单轮采样在维持质量方面更**高效且可靠**，而多轮方法可能需要额外保障措施以避免性能下降。  

---

### **4. 实践启示**
- **Grounded-T** 更适合维持**多样化教学策略**和**长期适应性**，因为它避免了向狭窄概念空间收敛。  
- **Intrinsic-T** 可能适用于短期任务，但随时间推移可能面临**多样性下降**和**过拟合**风险。  
- **单轮采样**建议用于保持一致性与质量，而多轮方法需谨慎设计以缓解性能差距。
