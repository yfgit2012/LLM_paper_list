## Summary
- **Objective**: To solve the problem of identifying the **first duplicate element** in an array of integers efficiently.  
- **Methodologies**: The paper employs a **set** data structure to track encountered elements. By iterating through the array and checking for existing entries in the set, the method ensures immediate detection of the first duplicate.  
- **Results**: The approach guarantees **O(n)** time complexity and **O(n)** space complexity, with successful validation through test cases (e.g., returning `4` for `[1, 2, 3, 4, 4, 5]` and `-1` for arrays with no duplicates).  
- **Key Contributions**: The primary contribution is the **simple and efficient use of a set** to solve the first-duplicate problem, offering a clear trade-off between time and space efficiency.  
- **Conclusions**: The method is effective for practical applications requiring quick detection of the first duplicate, demonstrating robustness across edge cases like multiple duplicates or entirely unique elements.
