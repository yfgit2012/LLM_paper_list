## Summary  
- **Objective**: To design and implement **STATe**, a system that generates structured reasoning steps and synthesizes final outputs for tasks requiring logical or domain-specific reasoning, such as argumentation, mathematics, and technical problem-solving.  
- **Methodologies**: The paper introduces a framework combining **action space design** (controllable dimensions like content, structure, style, and strategy), **synthesis modes** (strict, faithful, restructured, and conclusion synthesis), **prompt templates** (vanilla and internal reasoning modes), and **early stopping** to terminate redundant steps. Domain-specific taxonomies and iterative experimentation guide the system's adaptability.  
- **Results**: The framework enables flexible reasoning generation and synthesis, balancing fidelity, clarity, and usability. It supports multi-step tasks, structured outputs, and standalone final answers, validated through practical design guidance and trade-off analysis.  
- **Key Contributions**:  
  1. A structured approach to **action space design** with domain-specific controllable dimensions.  
  2. **Synthesis modes** offering varying levels of rephrasing and reorganization for different task requirements.  
  3. **Prompt templates** (vanilla and internal reasoning) to guide reasoning steps and internal logic.  
  4. **Early stopping** to prevent redundancy and ensure user-focused outputs.  
  5. A framework adaptable to diverse domains, emphasizing iterative refinement and alignment with taxonomies.  
- **Conclusions**: STATe provides a robust, flexible, and domain-agnostic framework for structured reasoning and synthesis. Its success hinges on balancing control (via prefixes) and flexibility (via internal reasoning), alongside iterative design and task-specific synthesis modes. The system is particularly effective for tasks requiring strict formatting or multi-step reasoning.
