### **Key Findings & Analysis**
1. **Model Exploration Strategies**:
   - **GPT-5.2**:
     - **"Finding-gate" Strategy**: Detects doorways by rotating until a doorway is identified, then navigates toward it. This method maximizes environmental coverage.
     - **Systematic Sweeping**: Systematically explores areas, ensuring all target objects are identified before terminating the process.
   - **Gemini-3 PRO**:
     - Similar "door-finding" and sweeping strategies in vision environments, but with a focus on vision-based navigation.
   - **Claude-4.5 Sonnet**:
     - No clear exploration pattern, suggesting less structured or randomized behavior.

2. **Performance in Text vs. Vision Environments**:
   - **Text-based environments** showed stronger performance for GPT-5.2 compared to vision-based ones. This may stem from the structured input (textual descriptions) versus the complexity of visual perception and object detection in vision tasks.

3. **Exploration Patterns**:
   - **Redundant Checks**: Some models (e.g., Gemini-3 PRO) exhibit repetitive checks, potentially leading to inefficiencies.
   - **Omission Risk**: Early detection of a door (e.g., in GPT-5.2) might cause the agent to skip further exploration, risking incomplete environmental discovery.

4. **Task Examples**:
   - **Route-Level Tasks**: Involving navigation (e.g., "Jump to the object at front-right") and spatial reasoning (e.g., "Mental Rotation" tasks requiring object ordering during 360-degree rotation).
   - **Survey-Level Tasks**: Assessing relative positions (e.g., "Location2View" and "View2Location" tasks) and cognitive mapping.

5. **Analysis Tools**:
   - **Visualization Platforms**: Metrics for active exploration (e.g., number of actions, coverage efficiency) in both text and vision worlds.
   - **Cognitive Maps**: Turn-by-turn visualizations of how models track objects and update their understanding of the environment.

### **Implications**
- **Model Design**: The study highlights the importance of structured exploration strategies (e.g., systematic sweeping) for efficient task completion, especially in complex environments.
- **Environment Complexity**: Vision-based tasks may require more robust perception and decision-making mechanisms, while text-based tasks benefit from explicit, structured input.
- **Future Work**: The analysis suggests opportunities to refine exploration algorithms, reduce redundancy, and improve adaptability across different task types.

## 中文翻译

### **关键发现与分析**
1. **模型探索策略**：
   - **GPT-5.2**：
     - **"门查找"策略**：通过旋转直到识别门，然后朝向门移动。此方法最大化环境覆盖。
     - **系统性扫视**：系统性探索区域，确保所有目标物体被识别后才终止过程。
   - **Gemini-3 PRO**：
     - 在视觉环境中采用相似的"门查找"和扫视策略，但侧重于基于视觉的导航。
   - **Claude-4.5 Sonnet**：
     - 无明显探索模式，表明行为可能更随机或结构不清晰。

2. **基于文本与视觉环境的性能**：
   - **基于文本的环境**中，GPT-5.2表现优于基于视觉的环境。这可能源于结构化输入（文本描述）与视觉任务中复杂视觉感知和物体检测的对比。

3. **探索模式**：
   - **冗余检查**：某些模型（如Gemini-3 PRO）存在重复检查，可能导致效率低下。
   - **遗漏风险**：早期检测到门（如GPT-5.2）可能导致代理跳过进一步探索，从而造成环境发现不完整。

4. **任务示例**：
   - **路线级任务**：涉及导航（如"跳向右前方的物体"）和空间推理（如"心理旋转"任务，要求在360度旋转中对物体进行排序）。
   - **调查级任务**：评估相对位置（如"位置到视角"和"视角到位置"任务）和认知地图构建。

5. **分析工具**：
   - **可视化平台**：在文本和视觉环境中，用于主动探索的指标（如动作数量、覆盖效率）。
   - **认知地图**：分步骤可视化模型如何追踪物体并更新对环境的理解。

---

### **启示**
- **模型设计**：研究强调了结构化探索策略（如系统性扫视）在复杂环境中高效完成任务的重要性。
- **环境复杂性**：基于视觉的任务可能需要更强大的感知和决策机制，而基于文本的任务则受益于显式、结构化输入。
- **未来工作**：分析表明，可通过优化探索算法、减少冗余并提升不同任务类型下的适应性来改进模型性能。
