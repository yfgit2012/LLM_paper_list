## Summary
- **Objective**: To address the issue of inductive copying in language models by exploring strategies that mitigate this behavior, with a focus on the HAPAX method.  
- **Methodologies**: The study employs cosine similarity and prefix-matching scores to measure inductive copying, evaluates direct suppression methods (e.g., reinitialization, loss penalties), and introduces HAPAX as an incentive-based approach. Experimental validation includes McNemar’s test for statistical rigor.  
- **Results**: Direct suppression methods fail to effectively reduce inductive copying as models adapt by creating alternative copying strategies. HAPAX significantly reduces induction heads and copying without compromising task performance. Inductive copying and abstractive performance are loosely coupled, with reduced copying not necessarily improving complex tasks.  
- **Key Contributions**: Introduces HAPAX as an incentive-based method to suppress inductive copying, demonstrating its superiority over direct suppression techniques. Highlights the decoupling of copying and abstractive performance, and emphasizes the importance of incentive design in model behavior.  
- **Conclusions**: Incentive-based approaches like HAPAX are more effective in mitigating inductive copying than direct behavior suppression. Reducing copying does not guarantee improved task performance, underscoring the need for refined incentive mechanisms in future work.

- **关键贡献**：提出HAPAX作为基于激励的方法以抑制归纳性复制，证明其优于直接抑制技术。强调复制与抽象生成性能的解耦，并突出激励设计对模型行为的重要性。
