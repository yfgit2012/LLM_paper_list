The **TRI-SYSTEM THEORY OF COGNITION** integrates three distinct cognitive processes to explain decision-making and reasoning, building on dual-process theory (System 1: intuitive, System 2: analytical) by adding a third system focused on **metacognitive control** or **emotional/affective regulation**. Below is a structured breakdown of the theory, supported by key references:

---

### **1. System 1: Intuitive/Emotional Processing**
- **Function**: Fast, automatic, and often unconscious reasoning driven by heuristics, gut feelings, or emotional responses.
- **Supporting References**:
  - **Zajonc (1980)**: Argues that preferences (intuition) can arise without deliberate inference, emphasizing the role of affective responses.
  - **Shiv & Fedorikhin (1999)**: Highlight the conflict between heart (emotions) and mind (cognition) in consumer decisions, showing how intuitive judgments dominate.
  - **Risko & Gilbert (2016)**: Discuss **cognitive offloading**, where intuitive judgments are prioritized when cognitive load is high, aligning with System 1's role.

---

### **2. System 2: Analytical/Logical Reasoning**
- **Function**: Slow, effortful, and conscious reasoning involving logical analysis, problem-solving, and rule-based processing.
- **Supporting References**:
  - **Sloman (1996)**: Proposes dual systems (System 1 and System 2) to explain reasoning, emphasizing the interplay between intuitive and analytical processes.
  - **Stanovich & West (2000)**: Investigate individual differences in reasoning, linking System 2 efficiency to rationality and metacognitive awareness.
  - **Thompson et al. (2011)**: Explore how reason and intuition interact, with System 2 providing oversight to correct intuitive biases.

---

### **3. System 3: Metacognitive/Emotional Regulation**
- **Function**: Governs awareness of one’s own cognitive processes, emotional regulation, and decision-making quality. It evaluates the outputs of Systems 1 and 2, ensuring alignment with goals and context.
- **Supporting References**:
  - **Thompson et al. (2011)**: Emphasize **metacognition** as a critical component, enabling individuals to monitor and adjust intuitive or analytical responses.
  - **Stanovich (2009)**: Highlights the role of **metacognitive skills** in overcoming cognitive biases, suggesting a third system for reflective control.
  - **Savine et al. (2010)**: Link motivational states (approach/avoidance) to cognitive control, implying System 3’s role in modulating attention and effort.

---

### **Key Implications and Applications**
1. **Integration of Dual-Process Theory**: The tri-system model extends dual-process theory by explicitly recognizing metacognitive oversight (System 3), addressing gaps in explaining how decisions are refined or corrected.
2. **AI and Decision-Making**: Recent studies (e.g., **Spatharioti et al., 2025**) explore how **LLM-based search** interacts with these systems, potentially overloading System 2 or relying on System 1 for rapid judgments.
3. **AI Literacy and Receptivity**: **Tully et al. (2025)** suggest that lower AI literacy correlates with greater receptivity to AI, reflecting System 1’s dominance in untrained individuals.
4. **Emotional Regulation**: **Zajonc (1980)** and **Shiv & Fedorikhin (1999)** underscore the role of affect in shaping intuitive judgments, which System 3 must balance with analytical rigor.

---

### **Conclusion**
The **TRI-SYSTEM THEORY OF COGNITION** provides a nuanced framework for understanding decision-making by integrating intuitive, analytical, and metacognitive processes. This model is supported by a range of studies, from classical dual-process theory (Sloman, 1996) to modern explorations of AI interaction (Spatharioti et al., 2025). By emphasizing the interplay between these systems, the theory addresses complexities in human cognition that dual-process models alone cannot fully capture.

## 中文翻译

**认知的三系统理论**整合了三种不同的认知过程以解释决策和推理，是在双系统理论（系统1：直觉，系统2：分析）的基础上增加的第三种系统，该系统专注于**元认知控制**或**情绪/情感调节**。以下是该理论的结构化解析，支持关键参考文献：

---

### **1. 系统1：直觉/情感处理**
- **功能**：快速、自动且通常无意识的推理，由启发式思维、直觉或情感反应驱动。
- **支持参考文献**：
  - **Zajonc（1980）**：认为偏好（直觉）可以在没有刻意推理的情况下产生，强调情感反应的作用。
  - **Shiv & Fedorikhin（1999）**：指出消费者决策中情感（心）与认知（脑）之间的冲突，显示直觉判断占主导地位。
  - **Risko & Gilbert（2016）**：讨论**认知卸载**，即在认知负荷较高时，直觉判断会被优先使用，这与系统1的作用一致。

---

### **2. 系统2：分析/逻辑推理**
- **功能**：缓慢、费力且有意识的推理，涉及逻辑分析、问题解决和基于规则的处理。
- **支持参考文献**：
  - **Sloman（1996）**：提出双系统（系统1和系统2）以解释推理，强调直觉与分析过程的相互作用。
  - **Stanovich & West（2000）**：研究推理中的个体差异，将系统2的效率与理性及元认知意识联系起来。
  - **Thompson等（2011）**：探讨理性与直觉的相互作用，系统2提供监督以纠正直觉偏差。

---

### **3. 系统3：元认知/情感调节**
- **功能**：管理个体对自身认知过程的意识、情感调节和决策质量，评估系统1和系统2的输出，确保其与目标和情境一致。
- **支持参考文献**：
  - **Thompson等（2011）**：强调**元认知**作为关键组成部分，使个体能够监控和调整直觉或分析反应。
  - **Stanovich（2009）**：突出**元认知技能**在克服认知偏差中的作用，建议引入第三系统以实现反思性控制。
  - **Savine等（2010）**：将动机状态（接近/回避）与认知控制联系起来，暗示系统3在调节注意力和努力中的作用。

---

### **关键启示与应用**
1. **双系统理论的整合**：三系统模型通过明确承认元认知监督（系统3）扩展了双系统理论，弥补了解释决策如何被优化或修正的不足。
2. **人工智能与决策**：近期研究（如**Spatharioti等，2025**）探讨了基于**大型语言模型（LLM）的搜索**如何与这些系统互动，可能过度负荷系统2或依赖系统1进行快速判断。
3. **人工智能素养与接受度**：**Tully等（2025）**指出，较低的人工智能素养与更高的接受度相关，反映了未受训练个体中系统1的主导地位。
4. **情感调节**：**Zajonc（1980）**和**Shiv & Fedorikhin（1999）**强调情感在塑造直觉判断中的作用，系统3需在分析严谨性与情感反应之间取得平衡。

---

### **结论**
**认知的三系统理论**通过整合直觉、分析和元认知过程，为理解决策提供了细致的框架。该模型受到从经典双系统理论（Sloman，1996）到现代人工智能互动研究（Spatharioti等，2025）的广泛支持。通过强调这些系统的相互作用，该理论解决了双系统模型无法完全捕捉的人类认知复杂性。
