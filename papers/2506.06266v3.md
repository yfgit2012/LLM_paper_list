## Summary
- **Objective**: To establish rigorous error bounds for a model's error terms and ensure the preservation of inner products between vectors, guaranteeing reliable decoding and robustness against errors.  
- **Methodologies**: Inductive reasoning, mathematical inequalities (e.g., triangle inequality), and bounding techniques to analyze error propagation. The preservation of inner products is achieved through decoding steps with constraints on parameters like $ \epsilon $ and $ \alpha $.  
- **Results**: The error terms $ \Delta_i^{(t)} $ are bounded by $ \sum_{a=1}^{m-1} (m-1)\epsilon^a $, with convergence ensured when $ \epsilon < \frac{1}{m^2} $. Inner products are preserved under decoding if $ \alpha < \frac{1}{m + 1} $, ensuring correct vector identification.  
- **Key Contributions**:  
  1. An inductive proof for error bounds in iterative models.  
  2. Formulation of convergence conditions linking $ \epsilon $ and $ m $.  
  3. A mechanism to preserve inner products during decoding, critical for applications like neural networks.  
- **Conclusions**: The model’s error is tightly controlled through parameter constraints, ensuring robustness and accurate decoding. This provides a theoretical foundation for algorithms requiring stable vector operations in machine learning or signal processing.
