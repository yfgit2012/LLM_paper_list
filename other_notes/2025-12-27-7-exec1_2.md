## Feeling Invisible at Work? Get Attention With G.R.O.W.T.H.

**Summary:**  
The article discusses how invisibility at work often stems from a lack of attention from senior leaders, and provides a six-step framework (GROWTH) to become more visible to them. The framework emphasizes giving credit to others, engaging with key influencers, aligning with one’s highest values, committing to continuous learning, tracking progress regularly, and honing expertise to articulate knowledge effectively. The author argues that visibility is achieved by understanding and aligning with the priorities and values of senior leaders, fostering meaningful relationships, and focusing on self-growth and professional development.  

**Key Points:**  
- **G (Give Credit):** Visibility is achieved by giving credit to others, as it aligns with the values of senior leaders and fosters mutual respect.  
- **R (Reach Out to Influencers):** Identify and build equitable relationships with key influencers in the organization, not just based on formal hierarchies but on informal networks and influence.  
- **O (Operate from Highest Value):** Align your work and goals with your core values and priorities to enhance intrinsic motivation and authenticity.  
- **W (Work on Continuous Learning):** Effective learning involves applying knowledge to produce outcomes, not just consuming information. This requires a structured process beyond traditional education methods.  
- **T (Track Progress):** Regularly report progress (e.g., weekly) using leading metrics, not just lagging KPIs, to demonstrate consistent performance and advocate for yourself.  
- **H (Hone Expertise):** Articulate your expertise through frameworks and principles to ensure others understand and value your contributions.  
- **Personal Growth:** Career advancement is tied to personal growth. The author offers collaboration opportunities to help individuals develop self-awareness, leadership skills, and a deeper understanding of their values.  
- **Quotes and References:** Zig Ziglar’s quote on giving others what they want, Sun Tzu’s *Art of War* (knowing oneself and the enemy), and a call to action for a consultation with the author’s advisors.

#### Translation 

**摘要：**  
文章探讨了职场中隐形现象通常源于高层领导缺乏关注，并提供了一个六步框架（GROWTH），以帮助员工更有效地引起高层领导的注意。该框架强调：给予他人肯定、与关键影响者建立联系、与自身最高价值观保持一致、致力于持续学习、定期跟踪进展以及精炼专业知识以有效表达知识。作者认为，通过理解并契合高层领导的优先事项和价值观，建立有意义的关系，并专注于自我成长和职业发展，才能实现可见性。  

**要点：**  
- **G（给予肯定）：** 通过给予他人肯定来实现可见性，这符合高层领导的价值观并促进相互尊重。  
- **R（接触影响者）：** 识别并建立与组织中关键影响者的平等关系，不仅基于正式层级，还基于非正式网络和影响力。  
- **O（以最高价值观行事）：** 将工作和目标与核心价值观和优先事项对齐，以增强内在动力和真实性。  
- **W（持续学习）：** 有效的学习涉及将知识应用于产出成果，而不仅仅是获取信息。这需要超越传统教育方法的结构化流程。  
- **T（跟踪进展）：** 定期（例如每周）使用领先指标报告进展，而不仅仅是滞后式KPI，以展示持续表现并为自己发声。  
- **H（精炼专业能力）：** 通过框架和原则清晰表达专业能力，确保他人理解和重视你的贡献。  
- **个人成长：** 职业发展与个人成长密切相关。作者提供合作机会，帮助个人提升自我认知、领导力和对价值观的深入理解。
